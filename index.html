Hey, I'm Partha.

I work on problems where physics and machine learning collide. The big question I keep chasing: how do you build an AI model that doesn't just memorize patterns from data, but actually respects the laws of thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, and structural physics?

My day-to-day involves building Physics-Informed Neural Networks that solve partial differential equations without ever needing a mesh, training Random Forest ensembles on noisy experimental sensor data, and designing PiML pipelines that fuse sparse measurements with prior physical knowledge.

I find the most interesting problems at the edges — where traditional CFD solvers are too slow, pure ML is too data-hungry, and the right answer is somewhere in between.

05 — Physics-Informed ML

PiML Framework

The core idea: if you know the governing equations, why not encode them directly into the loss? The model is forced to learn solutions that satisfy physics — not just fit the data.

# PiML total loss L_total = λ₁·L_data + λ₂·L_physics # Data fidelity L_data = (1/N) Σ |ŷᵢ - yᵢ|² # PDE residual (physics) L_phys = (1/M) Σ |F(u, ∂u/∂x)|² # NS momentum constraint ∂u/∂t + (u·∇)u = -∇p/ρ + ν∇²u # Continuity ∇·u = 0 # Training loop for epoch in range(50000): loss = L_data + 0.1*L_phys loss.backward() optimizer.step()
1

Pick Your PDE

Choose the governing equation that describes your system — heat equation, NS, wave equation, etc.

2

Design Architecture

Build a deep network deep enough to approximate the solution surface over your domain.

3

Encode Physics in Loss

Compute PDE residuals at collocation points via automatic differentiation and add to training loss.

4

Fuse Multi-Fidelity Data

Combine cheap simulation data with expensive experimental measurements efficiently.

5

Validate Against Physics

Check that predictions satisfy conservation laws — not just the test set error.

06 — PINNs

Physics-Informed Neural Networks

My favorite area — training neural networks to be PDE solvers. No mesh. No time-stepping. Just a network learning to satisfy equations at millions of points simultaneously.

🔥 Heat Equation
∂T/∂t = α∇²T + Q/ρc
// transient heat conduction
BC: T(x,0) = T₀
∂T/∂n|_Γ = q_flux
💧 Navier-Stokes
ρ(∂u/∂t + u·∇u) = -∇p + μ∇²u
// incompressible flow
∇·u = 0
Re = ρUL/μ
📐 Linear Elasticity
∇·σ + f = 0
// equilibrium equation
σ = C:ε
ε = (∇u + ∇uᵀ)/2
⚡ Inverse Problem
min_θ,λ L(θ, λ)
// recover hidden params
λ = [ν, ρ, k, α, ...]
from sparse sensor data
